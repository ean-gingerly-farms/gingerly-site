How We Grow

Why we chose hydroponics, how our NFT system works, and what it means for the greens that end up on your plate.

Gingerly Farms/5 min read/March 2026

When people hear "hydroponic farm," they picture something industrial. Warehouses full of robots and blinking LEDs. The reality at Gingerly Farms is simpler, more deliberate, and a lot closer to the ground than you'd expect.

We grow leafy greens and specialty herbs without soil. Instead, our plants sit in nutrient-rich water that's carefully mixed, monitored, and recirculated. There's no runoff into local waterways, no soil-borne disease, and no guessing about what the plants are getting. Every element is measured.

Why Hydroponics

Charleston's growing season is generous, but it comes with heat, humidity, and unpredictable storms. Leafy greens are especially finicky, bolting in the summer heat, getting beaten down by afternoon rain, or inviting every pest in the county. Controlled-environment growing takes the weather out of the equation.

That means we harvest year-round. Same quality in January as in July. Same consistency whether it's 40 degrees out or 95. For the chefs and kitchens we supply, that reliability is the whole point.

Our System

We use a nutrient film technique (NFT) system for most of our greens. Thin channels carry a shallow stream of nutrient solution across the plant roots. It's simple, efficient, and lets us grow a lot of greens in a compact footprint.

Seeds start in rockwool cubes in our propagation area. Once they've developed a strong root system (usually about 10 to 14 days), they move into the NFT channels. From there, depending on the variety, it's another two to four weeks until harvest.

We monitor pH, electrical conductivity, dissolved oxygen, and temperature daily. Small adjustments early prevent big problems later. The system is simple by design: fewer moving parts means less to break and less to troubleshoot at 6am.

What This Means for the Plate

Because we harvest to order and deliver locally, our greens reach your kitchen within hours of being cut. No cross-country trucking, no weeks in cold storage. The difference shows up in texture, flavor, and shelf life.

Our petite green mixes are tender and vibrant because they're picked young and delivered fast. The herbs are aromatic because they haven't been sitting in a plastic clamshell for a week. It's not complicated. It's just what fresh tastes like when the supply chain is measured in miles, not time zones.

Still Learning

We're not pretending to have it all figured out. We're two growers building this thing one system at a time, testing what works in our climate and for our customers. Some experiments pay off. Some don't. That's the nature of farming, even when there's no dirt involved.

This is the first in a series of notes about what we're learning along the way. If you're curious about anything (the science, the business, the greens), reach out. We're always happy to talk about what we're growing.
