How We Grow

Our hydroponic setup, how it works, and why we chose this approach for growing in Charleston.

Gingerly Farms/5 min read/March 2026

When people hear "hydroponic farm," they picture something industrial. Warehouses full of robots and blinking LEDs. Our setup is a lot simpler than that.

We grow leafy greens and herbs without soil. The plants sit in nutrient-rich water that gets mixed, checked, and recirculated. No dirt, no runoff, and we know exactly what the plants are getting because we're the ones mixing it.

Why Hydroponics

Charleston's growing season is long, but it comes with brutal heat, humidity, and random storms. Greens bolt in the summer, get hammered by afternoon rain, and attract every bug in the county. Growing indoors takes all of that off the table.

That means we can harvest year-round. Same quality in January as in July. For the restaurants we work with, that consistency is what they care about most.

Our System

We use a nutrient film technique (NFT) system for most of our greens. Thin channels carry a shallow stream of nutrient solution across the plant roots. It's simple, efficient, and lets us grow a lot of greens in a compact footprint.

Seeds start in rockwool cubes in our propagation area. Once they've developed a strong root system (usually about 10 to 14 days), they move into the NFT channels. From there, depending on the variety, it's another two to four weeks until harvest.

We monitor pH, electrical conductivity, dissolved oxygen, and temperature daily. Small adjustments early prevent big problems later. The system is simple by design: fewer moving parts means less to break and less to troubleshoot at 6am.

What That Means for the Product

We harvest to order and deliver locally, so the greens reach your kitchen within hours of being cut. No cross-country trucking, no sitting in cold storage for a week. You can taste the difference and they last longer in the fridge.

Short supply chain, fresh product. Fewer miles between the grow room and the plate, and that shows up in the quality.

Still Learning

We don't have it all figured out. We're two guys building this one system at a time, learning as we go. Some experiments pay off, some don't. That's farming, even without the soil.

If you're curious about any of it, reach out. We're happy to talk about what we're working on.
